Big Joel's Safari provides Educational Field Trips for students of all ages.

  • We love to share facts about the animals and the facility.
  • Teaching is a tradition in the Clinger family. Both of Big Joel's parents were school teachers.
  • Joel has learned a thing or two over the years!
  • All students can have the chance to see, touch, and feed many animals up close.
  • Carefully read the cancellation policy, rules, guidelines, and tips for a great field trip.

Field Trips and Pricing:

  • Field trips are offered throughout most of the season.
  • Field trips are not available on weekends.
  • Groups of 60+ students/ participants: $5.00 per participant
  • Groups smaller than 60 students/participants: $6.00 per participant
  • The field trip admission fee includes entry to the park and animal feed.
  • ​Bus drivers and teachers: FREE

Field Trip Format:

  • Before entry, the staff will go over park rules and tips for a fun and safe visit with the entire group.
  • Learn fun facts about the zoo and more during the pressentation.
  • Leaders will be given a map to explore Big Joel's Safari and a bucket of animal feed for the students.
  • Generally having 8-10 students per chaperone works best.
  • Each map is different and starts each group in a different area to avoid overcrowding.
  • Students are given the most interaction with the animal residents.
  • There are many animals and different areas to feed them.
  • Avoid using all of the feed at one spot.
  • There are informational signs about each of animal.
  • C-onsult the staff for further questions.
  • Average journey through the park is 1 hour.
  • Handwash stations are near the front of the park.
  • Additional attractions are not included in the entrance fee.

Cancellation and Late Policy: PLEASE READ

  • Only Big Joel can authorize the cancellation of a field trip due to inclement weather.
  • Safety is our fist priority.
  • Don't hesitate to reach out if plans change.
  • No refunds or reschedulings are available for groups that are 30 minutes late and don't call ahead.
  • We will call before cancelling.
  • Arrive 15 minutes early.
  • Rescheduling is available if necessary depending on the situation.

Rules and Guidelines for Educators

  • Arrive 15 minutes early.
  • Refer to the cancellation and late policy.
  • GPS may not be reliable in the rural farm area.
  • Have written directions handy.
  • No Outside Food is allowed in the park.
  • Bring water.
  • The gift shop may not be open to purchase water depending on the day of field trip.
  • The group must pay in one transaction along with a copy of the Tax Exempt form.
  • Refunds are available for absent field-trip individuals that paid in advance.
  • The animal feed does not contain "peanut or tree nut" products, but may have had contact with equipment that does process nuts.
 ​

Rules for Participants and Chaperones

  • Students are required to stay with chaperones at all times.
  • Respect the animals. This means being quiet, calm, and no harassing the animals.
  • Follow the direction of all rules, signs, and staff members.
  • Only feed animals the food given and please read signs. Many of the animals have special diets.
  • Do not throw feed at animals or on the ground because it could make the animals sick.
  • Do not run. Walk on designated paths only.
  • Please do not litter.
  • Do not pick up or kick gravel. Anyone caught throwing rocks especially at the animals, will be asked to leave.
  • Please do not disturb flowers, plants, and free roaming animals.
  • Additional attractions are not included in the entrance fee.
  • Be courteous and respectful of other visitors.

Tips for a great start to the field trip

  • Please arrive 15 minutes early, or be considered late.
  • Communicate schedule changes with the transportation coordinator.
  • Please wait to use the restroom, until the end of the introductory presentation.
  • Bus drivers and teachers: FREE
  • Parents, chaperones, and students pay the same price.
  • Groups should contain one chaperone to every 8-10 students.
  • 8-10 students is ideal for sharing a feed bucket.
  • Please share the rules and guidelines with all teachers involved with the field trip.
